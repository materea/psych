    Biodata

    Presented in not more than 3 sentences (NAMGOTRAdIS)

    • Name:
    • Age: Corroborate with date of birth
    • Marital status:
    • Gender:
    • Occupation:
    • Tribe:
    • Religion:
    • Address:
    • Informant:

    Usually, the principal informant is the patient. If not, state the reason.

    The name(s), relationship to the patient, and length of acquaintance of any other person interviewed.

    Make a comment regarding the reliability of the information provided by informant(s) assessed on the following parameters:

    • Familiarity with the patient and length of stay with the patient
    • Degree of concern regarding the patient
    • Intellectual and observational ability of the informant

    Source of referral:

    • Formal or informal
    • State name of referrer
    • Brief reasons for referral
    • Where is the patient being seen

    Presenting Complaints

    Presenting Complaints - with duration in chronological order

    Complaints by Patient: If the patient has no complaint (probably due to absent insight), state it.

    Complaints by Other Informant(s):

    As much as possible, state the chief complaint as a verbatim quote.

    Make a list of presenting complaints as concise as possible :

    • Complaint 1
    • Complaint 2
    • Complaint 3
    • Complaint 4
    • Complaint 5

    History of Presenting Complaints

    Chronological Description of SECT:

    Symptoms of the Current Episode:

    • Describe how each symptom evolved ‚Äì duration, how and when it began, nature, severity, course (increasing/decreasing, constant/intermittent)
    • Ask & record symptoms that might have been expected but which are not present

    Refer to clerkship of symptoms in the appendix.

    Etiology of the Symptoms:

    • Ask for possible precipitants or triggers - temporal relationship between symptoms and any physical disorder, psychological, or social problem
    • Perpetuating and worsening factors ‚Äì that exacerbate symptoms
    • Protective and improving factors ‚Äì that alleviate symptoms ‚Äì like medication, support, coping skills, time of day

    R/o other diagnoses - refer to psychiatric review of symptoms..

    Complications of Current Symptoms

    • The nature and duration of any functional impairment - at school, work, in the family; patient's interests; interpersonal relationships; personal habits, and physical health
    • How others have reacted to the patient‚Äôs problem

    Treatment So Far:

    • Detailed analysis of treatment received in chronology ‚Äì dose, duration, adherence, outcome i.e., effects & side effects

    Past Psychiatric History

    Details of Contact with Psychiatrists or Previous Episode(s) of Psychiatric Illness:

    • In chronology, discuss the details of contact with psychiatrists or previous episode(s) of psychiatric illness from which the client completely recovered.
    • For each episode inquire about - date, age at the time of illness (especially the first episode), symptoms of the episode, duration, nature, location, diagnosis (by who?), mode of treatment (out-patient or in-patient treatment including adherence)
    • If in-patient treatment was received, state how long admission was for, how many admissions so far, and when the last admission was
    • Response to treatment & how long each episode lasted: E.g., ‚Äúhe did well on medications ‚Ä¶. and symptoms abated over 3 months‚Äù
    • Longest period of wellness in between episodes; explore the reason for this longer period of wellness
    • Level of functioning in between episodes ‚Äì personal, occupational, or educational, family, and other social context

    Suicide and Deliberate Self-Harm Attempts:

    • Discuss any history of suicide and deliberate self-harm attempts

    Previous Surgeries in Details:
