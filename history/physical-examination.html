    General Survey

    Start by taking height, weight, and body temperature of the patient. Then comment on:

    • Build
    • Posture
    • Skin color
    • Eruptions
    • Petechiae
    • Vitiligo
    • Spider Naevi
    • Nutrition
    • Oedema
    • Hair
    • Nails
    • Clubbing
    • Lymph nodes
    • Swelling
    • Deformities
    • Thyroid gland
    • Injuries
    • Scars on the whole body

    Cardiovascular System

    Start with taking the pulse of the patient. Look for at least radial and femoral pulse. Comment on:

    • Rate
    • Rhythm
    • Volume
    • Character
    • Arterial wall
    • Radio-radial and radio-femoral delay

    Take the blood pressure in the right upper arm in both supine and erect positions.

    Look for neck veins, any engorgement, and jugular venous pressure.

    Conduct a thorough inspection, palpation, and auscultation of the precordium. Comment on:

    • Position and force of cardiac impulse
    • Apex beat
    • Heart sounds
    • Murmurs, if any

    Respiratory System

    Bare the chest of the patient and go for:

    • Inspection (rate and character of respiration, chest wall movements)
    • Palpation (position of trachea, swelling, tenderness, fremitus)
    • Percussion (character of note, symmetry)
    • Auscultation (breath sounds, added sounds, if any)

    Abdomen

    Again on a bare abdomen, comment on:

    • Inspection (shape, distention, movements, veins, umbilicus, visible peristalsis)
    • Palpation (tenderness, rigidity, organomegaly, hernial orifices, genitalia)
    • Percussion (character of note, shifting dullness, fluid thrill)
    • Auscultation (peristaltic sounds, arterial bruits, succession splash)

    Central Nervous System

    Start with higher mental functions; look for:

    • Consciousness and comprehension
    • Attention and concentration
    • Orientation

    Examine cranial nerves one by one. Here one should be ready with all the apparatus required for cranial nerve examination:

    • For smell (soap, toothpaste, coffee, asafetida, ginger, etc.)
    • For vision (Ishiara Charts, Snellen‚Äôs chart, Jaguar chart/newspaper)
    • For fundus examination (ophthalmoscope)
    • For eye reflexes (cotton wisp, a torch)
    • For deep tendon reflexes (knee hammer)
    • For temperature testing (test tubes with hot and cold water)
    • For taste (salt, sugar, lemon and quinine solution, drinking water, cotton swabs, placards with names of different tastes)
    • For hearing (tuning forks of different frequencies)
    • For touch (a blunt pin)

    Proceed for examination of the motor system. Look for:

    • Strength
    • Bulk
    • Tone
    • Co-ordination of all major muscles
    • Any involuntary movements

    Sensory System

    Look for pain, touch, temperature, position, vibration in all possible dermatomes. In case of deficiency, try to find out the level of deficiency.

    Check for both deep tendon and superficial reflexes. Check for bilateral:

    • Biceps
    • Triceps
    • Supinator
    • Knee
    • Ankle reflexes
    • Plantar reflex
    • Abdominal reflex
    • Cremastric reflex

    Look for cortical sensations including:

    • Astereognosis
    • Graphesthesia
    • Two-point discrimination
    • Sensory inattention

    Examine signs of autonomic system instability. This includes a thorough examination of:

    • Skin (and its appendages)
    • Mucous membrane
    • Pulsations in extremities
    • Gland functions (sweat, salivary, lacrimatory)
    • Endocrine system
    • Postural fall in blood pressure
    • Other cardiovascular reflexes
    • Valsalva maneuver
    • Deep breath test
    • Genito-urinary functions
    • Skin reaction to various stimuli

    Cerebellar Signs

    Examine for cerebellar signs including:

    • Rebound phenomenon
    • Finger-nose test
    • Heel-shin test
    • Rapid alternating movements (dysdiadokokinesis)

    Check for signs of meningeal irritation:

    • Neck rigidity
    • Kernig‚Äôs sign
    • Brudzinski's Neck sign

    Make a comment on:

    • Handedness
    • Skull
    • Spine
    • Gait & stance

    The central nervous system will become all the more important in a neurological case workup. This will be of great help in clinically localizing the lesion in the brain or spinal cord.

    Results of Investigation

    If the patient has undergone any investigations prior to the current consultation, note the findings. This may include:

    • Blood investigations (blood counts, endocrinological profile, liver and kidney function tests, blood electrolyte levels, lipid profile, antibody levels)
    • X-ray of any body part
    • CT scan
    • MRI
    • EEG (Electroencephalogram)
    • EMG (Electromyogram)
    • Any other relevant investigation

    Physical Findings or Diagnosis:

    Based on the detailed physical examination and available investigations, reach a conclusion regarding physical findings, diagnosis, or potential differentials.
