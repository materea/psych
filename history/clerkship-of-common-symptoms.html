    Hearing Voices

    • First time it was heard
    • Occurring from external space ‚Äì heard from the ears or other body parts
    • State in which it is heard ‚Äì clear consciousness or in sleep
    • Describe the voices heard ‚Äì e.g., male or female; familiar or not
    • What the voices say
    • Do they talk directly to the patient or talk to each other about the patient in 3rd person?
    • Are the words experienced as commands, and does the patient feel they must be obeyed?
    • Can the voices be terminated by will?
    • How the experience makes the patient feel ‚Äì distressing or not
    • Reaction to the voices ‚Äì do they inform behavior
    • Course ‚Äì constant or intermittent, the same, increasing, or decreasing
    • Persistence ‚Äì occurring on most days of the week
    • Last time it was heard
    • What provokes the experience
    • Rule out hallucinatory experiences in other sensory modalities as well as functional or reflex hallucination

    Seeing Things

    • First time it occurred
    • State in which it occurs
    • Describe what is seen
    • Are they familiar images?
    • Can it be terminated by will?
    • How the experience makes the patient feel
    • Does the experience inform behavior?
    • Course: constant or intermittent, the same, increasing, or decreasing
    • Persistence: occurring on most days of the week
    • Provoking factor
    • Last time it occurred
    • Rule out other hallucinatory experiences

    Odd Belief

    • Duration
    • What is the belief
    • Basis for the belief ‚Äì how the patient came about the belief
    • Is belief persistent, fleeting, or half-formed ‚Äì are there times the patient can be convinced otherwise
    • Establish if belief is opposed to or in keeping with the individual‚Äôs cultural, religious, or educational background
    • Does the belief inform behavior?
    • Course
    • Rule out hallucinatory experiences and disorders of thought possession

    Poor Sleep

    • Normal sleep time prior to illness
    • Current sleep time
    • Sleep latency period (time between going to bed and falling asleep) prior to illness and currently
    • What the patient does while awake
    • Early morning awakening?
    • Intermittent awakening?
    • How satisfied with sleep on awakening ‚Äì refreshed or unrefreshed
    • Compensatory daytime sleep
    • Any triggers? ‚Äì Stimulants like coffee or alcohol
    • Recurrent dreams?
    • Sleep conditions ‚Äì alone or with a partner, comfort levels
    • Any recent change in sleeping conditions

    Low Mood

    • When it started
    • Persistence
    • Course
    • Does the patient still get happy with events or things that made them happy in the past?
    • Weepy spells?
    • Low energy, lack of interest in previously enjoyed activities
    • Feelings of hopelessness, worthlessness, pessimistic views of present, low self-esteem, changes in sleep, appetite (and weight)
    • Suicidal, homicidal, infanticidal ideations or attempts
    • Any triggers

    Low or Increased Energy

    • As evidenced by ‚Äì e.g., not been able to complete usual tasks or activities or undertaking several tasks at the same time.
    • Persistence
    • Course
    • Any delusion or command hallucination
    • Rule out depression, mania

    Talking Out of Context or Talking Irrelevantly

    • Cite example of what the patient says
    • Persistence
    • Course
    • When it occurs
    • Is the patient difficult to follow or content of speech inappropriate i.e., giving irrelevant answers to questions asked or deviating from the topic of discussion
    • Is talk excessive
    • Does the patient talk to self
    • Rule out hallucinations ‚Äì patient may be responding to hallucinatory experiences

    Keeping to Self (Social Withdrawal)

    • Duration
    • Persistence
    • Course
    • Describe how this is done ‚Äì e.g., keeps to self in the room all day‚Ä¶, would not join in conversations‚Ä¶, would not visit friends or relatives‚Ä¶
    • Is it active ‚Äì usually in response to delusions?

    • Establish that this is opposed to the patient‚Äôs usual behavior
    • Reaction to attempts at engagement ‚Äì irritability or anger, silence, etc.

    Assuming Unusual or Odd Postures

    • Describe what the patient does
    • First time it was observed or reported
    • Establish that it is opposed to usual behavior
    • Persistence
    • Course
    • Context in which it occurs ‚Äì e.g., while praying, or at any time
    • Rule out passivity phenomenon, hallucinations, catatonic features, seizures
    • Does the patient attach any significance to the postures assumed ‚Äì mannerism, stereotypy

    Dancing Unusually

    • Any specific pattern of dance or is dance in a disorganized manner
    • Was the patient singing as well or dancing to music playing
    • Duration of symptom
    • Persistence
    • Course
    • Does dance have any special meaning to the patient
    • Establish that it is not in keeping with usual behavior
    • Rule out affective symptoms ‚Äì unusual sadness, exaggerated feelings of self-importance; hallucinations; delusions

    Stripping Self

    • First time it occurred
    • Manner of stripping ‚Äì rips clothes off or pulls them up one after the other
    • Establish that it is opposed to usual behavior
    • To what extent the patient strips ‚Äì to underwear or totally naked
    • Course
    • Persistence
    • Context in which it occurs ‚Äì in the house, outside, etc.
    • Rule out passivity phenomenon and hallucinations

    Picking and Hoarding Items

    • Describe exactly how it is done
    • Are the items useful or rubbish?
    • Duration
    • Course
    • Persistence
    • Does the behavior have special meaning for the patient?
    • Where the patient keeps the item and how it is kept ‚Äì in an organized manner or patient makes a clutter
    • Any other disorganized behavior
    • Rule out hallucinations, passivity phenomenon
    • Rule out negative symptoms including self-care as symptoms in schizophrenia occur in complexes

    Wandering

    • When and how it started
    • Location and distance of wandering ‚Äì where patient was seen
    • Did the patient disclose destination ‚Äì where s/he was going
    • Did the patient say for how long s/he would be away?
    • Could the patient be reached while away e.g., on the phone?
    • What the patient was doing when seen e.g., talking to self, picking rubbish, etc.
    • Condition of patient when seen ‚Äì e.g., unkempt
    • Rule out hallucinations, delusions
